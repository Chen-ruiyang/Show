About me

My name is Chen Ruiyang, and I study Internet of Things Technology Application at Shanghai Southwest Engineering School. Looking back, a series of competitions have paved the way for my growth.

My competition journey wasn't always smooth sailing. In the first semester of my freshman year, I enthusiastically signed up for the Shanghai Vocational College Innovation and Creativity Competition, but reality gave me a cold shower—I only won the "Excellent Award." I was both frustrated and unwilling to accept defeat, but my rational mind told me: a low starting point doesn't mean a low finish line.

My sophomore year was a year of preparation and breakthrough. I began to use all my spare time to delve into professional skills. During the preparation process, I encountered and mastered a large amount of knowledge beyond the textbooks. To overcome technical difficulties, I self-studied and obtained certificates such as Alibaba Front-end Engineer and Artificial Intelligence Trainer; to improve my algorithmic logic, I participated in the Campus Skills Festival and won second prize in the Campus Skills Competition covering network fundamentals and Python algorithms. Meanwhile, to hone my logic and expression skills, I joined the school's campus debate club and actively participated in debate competitions, winning third place.

Hard work pays off, and I finally reaped the rewards. I returned to the competition arena, and this time, I not only won a bronze medal in the municipal vocational college competition, but also competed against many university students in the 2025 China International College Student Innovation Competition (Shanghai Division), achieving an excellent result of "two silver and one bronze" medals. From an award of excellence to a bronze medal and then to a silver medal, this was not only an improvement in awards, but also a testament to my technical skills and mental maturity.

Beyond the competition arena, I also love life. My sixth-place finish in the school's badminton competition is a testament to my vitality and perseverance. In the future, I will continue to carry this spirit of "never giving up and being willing to delve deeper" to embrace challenges in new fields.
